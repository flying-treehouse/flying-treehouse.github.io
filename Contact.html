Audition Information

Auditions for Flying Treehouse take place at the beginning of Fall Quarter each year, typically during the week after the Club Fair. Auditions are super simple and meant to be low stress!

The audition consists of three sections: 

A fun, zany, improv game!

First, you will be asked to join the current members in a fun, energetic warm-up game. What’s important here is to be engaged, have fun, and bring lots of enthusiasm!

A 5-minute teaching session.

Next, we’ll ask you to prepare a two-minute teaching demo. Past auditionees have taught anything from origami to how to shoot a goal in ice hockey to how to tie your shoe. The lesson can be anything you’re comfortable with, and we encourage you to find a way to make it interactive!

An improv performance of one of our past stories! 

Finally, we’ll give you a story written by a former third-grade student taught by veteran Flying Treehouse members. You’ll be given about 10 minutes to decipher the kid’s handwriting and act out their story however you see fit! This is the part where you can be most creative, so have fun with it! Auditions are truly meant to be low stress and a fun experience, so enjoy your time!
